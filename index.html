Understanding the landscape

Which jobs are at risk — and which aren't?

Research shows that AI disruption is uneven. Jobs built on routine, repetitive tasks face the highest risk. Jobs requiring human judgment, creativity, and social skills are far more resilient.

⚠ Higher Risk
Data Entry & Processing Very High

Highly repetitive tasks AI can already do faster and cheaper.

Basic Customer Service High

Chatbots and virtual assistants already handle many routine inquiries.

Basic Accounting & Bookkeeping High

Automated tools are replacing routine financial tasks.

Assembly Line & Warehouse Work Medium-High

Robotics automation continues to expand into physical labor roles.

Routine Code Writing Medium

AI tools like Copilot can generate boilerplate code — but not full systems.

✓ More Resilient
Mental Health & Counseling Low Risk

Empathy, trust, and human connection cannot be automated.

Teaching & Education Low Risk

Mentorship, classroom judgment, and student relationships stay human.

Healthcare & Nursing Low Risk

AI assists diagnosis but hands-on care remains deeply human.

Creative Design & Strategy Low Risk

Original vision, cultural context, and human taste remain hard to replicate.

AI & Tech Development Growing

Someone has to build, maintain, and evaluate the AI systems. That's you.

Research by Frank & Ahn (2024) published in PNAS Nexus found that AI exposure predicts unemployment risk based on the types of tasks in a job — not the job title itself. The real question is: does your work require judgment, creativity, or human connection? If so, you're in a much stronger position.

Future-proofing yourself

Skills that still matter — and always will

The goal isn't to out-compete AI. It's to develop skills that AI can't easily replicate, and to learn how to work alongside it effectively.

🧠

Critical Thinking

The ability to evaluate information, spot flaws in AI-generated output, and make nuanced judgments is more valuable than ever. AI produces answers — you decide if they're right.

Always in demand
🤝

Human Communication

Negotiating, leading, collaborating, and connecting emotionally are things AI cannot do. Strong communication skills set you apart in any field.

Hard to automate
💡

Creativity & Problem-Solving

AI remixes patterns from existing data. Truly original ideas, novel solutions, and creative strategies still require a human mind.

Growing demand
🔧

AI Collaboration

Learning how to prompt, evaluate, and work with AI tools is a skill itself. People who can guide AI effectively are significantly more productive.

Emerging skill
📊

Data Literacy

Understanding how to read data, identify patterns, and question assumptions helps in nearly every field — from business to healthcare to education.

Cross-industry
🔄

Adaptability

The most resilient workers are lifelong learners. The specific tools will keep changing — your ability to learn new ones is what lasts.

Future-proof
